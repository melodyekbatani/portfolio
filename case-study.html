Mobile App Redesign

Streamlining the onboarding experience for a fintech application

Role UX/UI Designer
Duration 3 Months
Result +34% User Retention

Overview

The original onboarding flow for this fintech mobile application was experiencing high user drop-off rates during the account creation process. Users reported confusion about required information and a lack of visual feedback on their progress.

This case study outlines how we redesigned the onboarding experience to reduce friction, increase clarity, and ultimately improve user retention by 34%.

The Problem

  • 45% abandonment rate during account creation
  • Unclear information architecture with no progress indication
  • Poor mobile experience with form fields not optimized for touch
  • Lack of error handling causing user frustration
  • Insufficient trust signals to encourage form completion

Research & Discovery

We conducted user interviews with 15 existing users and 10 individuals who had abandoned the process. Key findings revealed:

Mobile Friction

Users struggled with entering personal information on small screens and switching between keyboard and form fields.

Unclear Requirements

Many users didn't understand why certain information was required or how it would be used.

Lack of Feedback

No indication of progress left users unsure if they were on the right track or how many steps remained.

Trust Concerns

Users were hesitant to provide sensitive financial information without clear security validations.

Solution Design

Based on our research, we implemented the following changes:

1. Progressive Form Steps

Broke the lengthy form into 4 manageable steps with a clear progress indicator, reducing cognitive load and giving users a sense of achievement at each step.

2. Mobile-Optimized Inputs

Implemented context-specific keyboards, larger touch targets, and mobile-first field design principles for better usability.

3. Inline Validation & Feedback

Provide real-time validation with helpful error messages and success states, eliminating surprise validation failures at submission.

4. Trust Building Elements

Added certification badges, security information, and privacy confirmations to increase user confidence in the process.

Results & Impact

Post-launch analytics showed significant improvements:

34% Increase in user retention
42% Reduction in form abandonment
2.1 min Average completion time (down from 5.3 min)
4.8/5 User satisfaction rating

Key Learnings

  • Progressive disclosure is powerful — Breaking complex processes into manageable steps significantly reduces cognitive load and abandonment.
  • Mobile-first design matters — Designing for the smallest screen first ensures better experiences across all devices.
  • Real-time feedback builds trust — Users are more confident when they receive immediate validation and clear error messages.
  • Transparency increases confidence — Explaining why information is needed and how it's protected encourages users to complete sensitive forms.
  • Iteration based on data is essential — User research and testing should continuously inform design decisions.
